# VocaBoost — TA Support Guide

A quick, plain-language guide for handling the most common student issues from your **teacher account**.
Good news: after recent updates, most issues now fix themselves or you can handle them directly —
you only need to send a few specific cases to me (David), listed at the end.

## 4 things to know first

1. **"Grading Failed" usually fixes itself now.** If a student hits a grading error from a network
   hiccup, the app recovers their grade automatically. Have them wait a moment, tap the button, or
   reload the page — it should resolve. Only send it to me if it keeps failing *after a fresh reload*.

2. **You can now accept disputed answers yourself** — no more waiting on me to fix it. (See issue #1.)

3. **After you change a student's grade, they must REFRESH.** When you accept a challenge or change a
   setting, the student's screen does **not** update on its own. Tell them to reload the page (a normal
   refresh) to see the new score or unlocked day.

4. **Don't add or remove lists from classes.** Every class is already set up with the right lists
   (Ascent, Summit, etc.) — students just pick the one they want from the selector on their dashboard.
   Changing list assignments can knock students off their progress, so if a class's lists ever look
   wrong, **send it to me** instead of changing them.

## Common issues → what to do

### 1. "A word was marked wrong but it's actually right" (disputed grade)
**The most common issue — and you can now fix it yourself.**
1. Ask the student to tap **Challenge** next to that answer on their results screen, and submit it.
2. Go to **Gradebook** → open that student's test → find the pending challenge.
3. Click **Accept** if you agree (or **Reject** if you don't).
4. Accepting marks that answer correct, recalculates the score, and — if it brings them to passing —
   advances their day automatically.
5. Tell the student to **refresh** to see the update.

*Note: rejecting costs the student a challenge token **until the next weekly reset (every Monday)**, so only reject if it's genuinely wrong.*

### 2. "Grading Failed" / the test won't grade
- Have them **reload the page and submit again.** The app now recovers from network hiccups, so this
  usually works on its own.
- If the message says something like *"please reload and submit again,"* that **is** the right action —
  have them reload.
- **Send it to me only if it keeps failing after a fresh reload.**

### 3. "My progress reset to Day 1" / "My list disappeared"
- Their progress isn't lost — the right list just needs to be showing for them.
- First, have them check the **class/list selector** on their dashboard and pick the correct list.
- If that doesn't bring their progress back → **send it to me.** (Don't add or remove lists yourself —
  see the note below; the classes are already set up correctly.)

### 4. "The day won't advance" / "I'm stuck after a test"
- First check: did they actually **pass**? (Each class has a pass mark, e.g. 92%.) If they're just
  below, they simply need to **retake** — that's normal, not a bug.
- If a disputed answer is what's keeping them under the mark → handle it as **#1 (accept the challenge)**.
- If they **passed** but it didn't move on → have them **reload** first.
- Still stuck on a passed test after reloading → **send it to me.**

### 5. "I'm stuck on the loading screen"
- Have them try a **different device or browser** and a fresh reload.
- If it sticks on one specific day → **send it to me** (note which day, and ask for a screenshot).

### 6. Student on the wrong list / keeps landing on the wrong one
- The right lists are already on every class — students just pick the one they want from the
  **class/list selector** on their dashboard. Have them select the correct list there.
- If one specific student needs a different *default* list → **send it to me.**

### 7. "New words stopped showing" / "새 단어가 안 떠요" (only getting reviews)
- This is the app **holding them on review because their recent review scores are low** (roughly under 30%). It's working as
  designed, not a freeze.
- **Tell the student:** *"Keep doing the reviews and get your scores up (above ~30%) — new words come back automatically."*
  Skipping or rushing reviews keeps new words locked.
- If a teacher wants to force new words for a specific student anyway → **send me their email.**

### 8. "Grades → 'no results'" for a student who's clearly active
- Known Gradebook **display** bug for students inactive ~a month — their grades exist, they just don't show on the first page.
- **Workaround:** in Gradebook, filter by **Class + a Date range** covering when they last tested (or page forward). No fix needed.

## Switching students to SUMMIT (after they finish ASCENT)

ASCENT has **1,600 words**. A student finishes it once all 1,600 have been introduced:
- **ADV classes (80 words/day): after Day 20** → switch them to SUMMIT on **Day 21**.
- **FINAL classes (100 words/day): after Day 16** → switch them to SUMMIT on **Day 17**.

(SUMMIT has 800 words → about **8 days** at 100/day, **10 days** at 80/day.)

**How to switch:** SUMMIT is **already added to every class** — you don't assign anything. When a student
is ready, have them pick **SUMMIT** from the class/list selector on their dashboard. SUMMIT starts fresh
at its own Day 1 — that's expected for a new list.

**Cue they're ready:** a student who has finished ASCENT stops getting *new* words (review only). That's
the signal it's time to have them pick SUMMIT from the selector.

## Students moved from ADV to FINAL (same ASCENT list)

Two things change for these students — set expectations accordingly:

1. **Daily amount goes from 80 → 100 words/day** (FINAL's pace). Same list, bigger daily chunk.
2. **Their progress now carries over automatically** — a student moved into FINAL (or any class change on
   the same list) keeps their ASCENT day. If the first screen still shows **Day 1**, have them **reload once**
   — it should correct. Only if it's **still** Day 1 after a reload, send me their email + the day they should
   be on, and I'll carry it across.

## When to send it to me
Message me with the student's **email + class + which day**, and a **screenshot** if possible, for:
- A student **moved from ADV to FINAL** (or any class change) whose progress is **still Day 1 after a reload** —
  tell me the day they should be on and I'll carry it over right away.
- "Grading Failed" that persists **after a fresh reload**.
- Stuck on a **passed** test/day that reloading doesn't fix.
- Stuck on the **loading screen** for a specific day.
- Setting a specific student's **default list**.
- Anything that looks like **wrong or missing data** (scores that don't add up, duplicate entries).
